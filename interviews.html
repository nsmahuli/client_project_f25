Interviewing

Turn Interviews into Opportunities

Every interview is more than a test—it’s a conversation and a gateway to exciting possibilities.

Interviews allow you to show how your UMSI experiences set you apart, demonstrate your problem-solving skills, and convey your enthusiasm for the work.

No matter your comfort level, preparation is the best way to ensure you communicate your value and make a lasting impression.

Interview Prep Checklist

  1. Get to Know the Employer Dig into the organization’s work, values, and recent projects. Familiarize yourself with their mission and think about how you can contribute.
  2. Reread the Job Description Highlight skills and qualifications they’re looking for. Draft a few stories from your UMSI experience that illustrate those skills in action.
  3. Prepare Your Storytelling Toolkit Don’t just list responsibilities—explain your impact. Use frameworks like STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to paint a vivid picture of your accomplishments.
  4. Practice Different Interview Types Phone, virtual, and in-person interviews each have unique rhythms. Try simulating each format with a friend or career coach.
  5. Ready Your Questions Thoughtful questions show you’re both engaged and evaluating the fit for yourself. Ask about team dynamics, challenges, or growth opportunities.
  6. Plan Your Logistics Choose your outfit, test your technology, and map your route or set up your interview space.

Networking FAQ

  • What if my resume doesn’t match every qualification?
    Focus on what you offer—your ability to learn, adapt, and contribute. Interviews are your chance to highlight transferable skills and how quickly you pick up new concepts.
  • What’s the best way to respond to “Tell me about a challenge you’ve faced”?
    Pick a story where you grew or made a difference, not just the biggest problem you’ve faced. End it with what you learned or how you’ll apply that lesson going forward.
  • How can I calm my nerves before and during the interview?
    Practice aloud, visualize a positive outcome, and remind yourself that the interviewer wants you to succeed. Take slow breaths, smile, and take pauses as needed.
  • How soon should I follow up after an interview?
    Send a thank you note within 24 hours—mention specifics from your conversation and reiterate your enthusiasm.
  • How can I handle tough or unexpected questions?
    It’s perfectly okay to take a breath and think. If you don’t know an answer, be honest and explain how you’d go about finding a solution.
  • What supports does UMSI offer for interviews?
    From one-on-one coaching and practice sessions to interview workshops and resource guides, the CDO is here to help you every step of the way.

You’ve worked hard to get to this stage—now let your skills and personality shine. Every interview is a learning opportunity. With solid preparation and a positive attitude, you’re ready for whatever comes next!
