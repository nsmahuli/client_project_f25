Networking & Building Connections

Grow Your Network, Grow Your Career

Networking is about more than just finding a job. It’s about building authentic relationships,learning from others’ experiences, and discovering new opportunities across the information field.

Whether you’re an introvert or an extrovert, just starting out or looking to expand your connections, UMSI resources can help you feel more comfortable and strategic about networking—on campus and beyond, both online and face-to-face.

Not sure where to start or want to discuss strategies? Meet with a CDO Career Coach to talk through your networking plan.

Steps to Effective Networking

  1. Identify Your Goals: Are you seeking career advice, exploring new fields, or interested in specific organizations?
  2. Build Your List: Start with classmates, alumni, faculty, LinkedIn contacts, and members of student organizations.
  3. Reach Out: Use email, LinkedIn, or in-person events to make introductions. Reference shared connections or interests.
  4. Prepare for Conversations: Research each contact and prepare questions. Be ready to share about yourself.
  5. Follow Up: Always send a thank you. Stay in touch by sharing updates or relevant information.

Networking FAQ

  • I feel awkward reaching out to people. How do I get started?
    Start with those you have something in common with—classmates, UMSI alumni, or friends of friends. Using a simple script can make initial outreach easier.
  • What should I include in my first message?
    Introduce yourself, reference a shared connection or interest, state your purpose for reaching out (such as learning about their career journey), and request a brief conversation.
  • How do I make networking less transactional?
    Focus on learning and relationship building—not just asking for favors or jobs. Be curious, listen well, and offer your thanks and insights where you can.
  • What’s the best way to keep in touch?
    Follow up after conversations with a thank you, and periodically share updates on your progress, or send interesting articles or event invitations.
  • Is virtual or in-person networking more valuable?
    Both are useful! Take advantage of campus events for in-person connections and use LinkedIn or email to expand your network globally.
  • Can the CDO help me meet new people?
    Yes. The CDO team can recommend contacts, host networking events, and help you strategize your outreach.
